The Rule of Ramses II

Ramses II, often called Ramses the Great, was one of Ancient Egypt's most powerful and celebrated pharaohs. His reign marked a golden era for Egypt, characterized by monumental architecture, military campaigns, and a flourishing culture. Known for his ambitious building projects, Ramses II commissioned the construction of temples, statues, and cities that showcased the grandeur of his rule.

A key highlight of Ramses' reign was the signing of the first known peace treaty in history with the Hittites after the Battle of Kadesh. His leadership and vision left a lasting legacy, influencing Egypt and the world for generations.
